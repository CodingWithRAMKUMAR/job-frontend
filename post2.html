Resume Tips for IT Freshers: What Recruiters Actually Look For

📅 Published: April 11, 2026 | 5 min read

Your resume has about 6 seconds to impress a recruiter. Here's what actually works in 2026 based on feedback from HR professionals at Indian IT companies.

1. Keep It Under 1 Page

Freshers don't need 2-page resumes. Remove 10th/12th grade percentages unless exceptional. Remove "References available upon request" (it's assumed). Keep only relevant information.

2. Put Skills at the Top

Right after your name and contact info, list your technical skills. Recruiters scan for keywords like Python, Java, SQL, React, AWS. Make them easy to find.

3. Showcase Projects, Not Just Courses

List 2-3 projects with: tech stack used, what problem it solves, link to GitHub/live demo. Example: "E-commerce website using React + Node.js - 500+ users in first month"

4. Add Internships (Even Small Ones)

Any work experience counts - virtual internships, freelancing, college projects for real clients. Focus on what you accomplished, not just what you did.

5. Remove These Immediately

❌ "Objective: Seeking a challenging position..." (outdated)
❌ Soft skills like "hardworking, team player" (everyone writes this)
❌ Hobbies unless relevant
❌ Photo (not needed in India for most IT roles)

6. Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with: Developed, Built, Created, Optimized, Automated, Reduced, Improved. Example: "Optimized database queries reducing load time by 40%"
